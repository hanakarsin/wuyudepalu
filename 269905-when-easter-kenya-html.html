All the Easter Sunday celebrations and the other related celebrations like Palm Sunday and Ash Wednesday are all considered Christian feasts that have movable dates.

So, why does the date of Easter change but not Christmas? This is different from Christmas, which has a fixed date on the solar calendar. On the other hand, this festivity date depends on the lunar cycle of the Jewish calendar.

However, Western Christianity seems to have a more defined guide since they follow the Gregorian calendar. Their Easter holidays tend to fall on an exact day, which is on a Sunday, and the date will be between the 22nd of March and the 25th of April.

Eastern Orthodox Christianity uses the Julian Calendar; their Easter celebrations fall on a Sunday, and the date falls between the 4th of April and the 8th of May. This is slightly different from the dates in the Gregorian Calendar.

How are the Easter dates arrived at?

It is believed the crucifixion and death of Jesus Christ took place during the Jewish Passover. This Jewish religious celebration is usually held on the first full moon after the vernal equinox.

What determines when Easter is each year? Since the full moon varies depending on the different time zone, the Church opted to use the 14th day of the Lunar Moon rather than the Paschal Full Moon. They set the Easter Sunday date on the following day. Once the church determines the full moon's date, then all the other dates in the holiday's calendar can be determined.

What to do on the Easter holiday

The period before the holiday begins is very important for the Christians who are in the following groups: Protestant Denominations, Roman Catholicism and the Western Christianity group.

The Easter vigil is performed by many churches across the world, and it starts in the late hours of the Holy Saturday before Easter Sunday. Eastern Orthodox Christianity, do things a little different.

Their celebration rituals begin with them celebrating the Great Lent. Unlike Ash Wednesday, they celebrate Clean Monday, 40 days before Easter Sunday without including Sundays. They not only have a Palm Sunday instead, but they also have a Palm Week, which is the last week of the Great Lent.

The Lazarus Saturday comes immediately before Palm Sunday, and after celebrating Palm Sunday, they mark the beginning of the holy week.
